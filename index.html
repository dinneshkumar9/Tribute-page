Subhas Chandra Bose

Netaji Bose the forgotten hero who gave sacrificed his life for complete independence of India

Here's a timeline of Subhas Chandra Bose's life:

  • 1897 - In 23rd January 1897, Subhas Chandra Bose was born on Cuttack, Bengal Presidency, British India.
  • 1912 - Subhas Chandra Bose cleared his Matriculation Examination and secured Second Rank in the whole State.
  • 1913 - Subhas Chandra Bose was admitted to Presidency College, Calcutta. It was the most prestigious college in India.
  • 1916 - Subhas Chandra Bose was expelled from Presidency College for the allegation of beating a Professor of this college.
  • 1917 - Calcutta University permitted Subhas Chandra Bose to continue studying, but in another collage (SCC).
  • 1918 - Subhas Chandra Bose recieved his B.A degress in First Class with Honours in Philosophy and Ranked Second in CU.
  • 1919 - On 20 October 1919, Subhas Chandra Bose readied his application for Indian Civil Service on his father's urge.
  • 1920 - Subhas Chandra Bose secured Fourth Rank in the Indian Civil Service (ICS) examination which was rare for Indians.
  • 1921 - In April 1921, Subhas Chandra Bose take a firm oath that he won't join the ICS and will work for his Motherland.
  • 1923 - Subhas Chandra Bose elected as the President of All-India Youth Congress and Secretary of Bengal Congress.
  • 1925 - Subhas Chandra Bose was arrested by the British Indian Government and was sent to Mandalay Jail.
  • 1927 - In 1927 (After being released from Prison), Subhas Chandra Bose was elected as the Secretary of the All-India Congress.
  • 1928 - Subhas Chandra Bose become the GOC of Bengal Volunteers and Marched an Army into the Road of Calcutta.
  • 1930 - Subhas Chandra Bose was elected as the Mayor of Calcutta Corporation. He was elected when he was in Jail!
  • 1935 - Subhas Chandra Bose published first book - “The Indian Struggle”. It was published from England.
  • 1938 - Subhas Chandra Bose was elected as the President of the All-India Congress and decided to use Force against Britain.
  • 1939 - Subhas Chandra Bose was re-elected as the President of All-India Congress by a confrontation with Gandhi.
  • 1940 - Subhas Chandra Bose start to organize AIFB. He was already resigned from AIC and created his own party - AIFB.
  • 1941 - Subhas Chandra Bose decided to leave India and take help from Foreign Powers to make India Free.
  • 1943 - Rash Behari Bose handed over the charge of INA to Netaji and Netaji become the Supreme of INA.
  • 1945 - On 17th August 1945, Netaji was seen for the last time in Public. Since then, it has all been a Mystery till Today.

Slogans

S C Bose's most famous slogans/quotes are तुम मुझे खून दो मैं तुम्हें आजादी दूंगा ("Give me blood and I will give you freedom", Tum Mujhe khoon do main tumhe aazadi dunga), Dilli Chalo "On to Delhi!" This was the call he used to give to the INA army to encourage them. "Jai Hind" or "Glory to India!" was another slogan used by him, and later adopted by the Government of India and the Indian Armed Forces. Another slogan coined by him was "Ittefaq, Etemad, Qurbani" (Urdu for "Unity, Agreement, Sacrifice").
